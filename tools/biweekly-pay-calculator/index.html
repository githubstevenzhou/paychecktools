Frequently Asked Questions

Does this biweekly paycheck calculator include taxes?

Yes. This calculator estimates federal and state taxes using the percentages you enter. It does not automatically apply tax brackets, deductions, or credits, so results should be treated as estimates.

Is this calculator accurate for all US states?

The calculator works for any state as long as you provide the correct state tax rate. Local taxes, city taxes, and other deductions are not included.

How is overtime pay calculated?

Overtime pay is calculated separately based on the overtime hours you enter. The calculation assumes standard overtime rules, but actual rates may vary depending on employer policy and local labor laws.

Why is my net pay different from my actual paycheck?

Differences may occur due to benefits, retirement contributions, insurance premiums, garnishments, or tax withholding rules that are not included in this simplified estimate.

Can salaried employees use this biweekly calculator?

This tool is designed primarily for hourly workers. Salaried employees can still use it by converting their salary to an equivalent hourly wage, but results may be less precise.

How the Biweekly Paycheck Calculator Works

This biweekly paycheck calculator helps you estimate how much you take home every two weeks based on your hourly wage, hours worked, overtime, and tax rates.

Enter your hourly wage and total hours worked during the pay period. If you worked overtime, include those hours separately. A biweekly pay schedule typically results in 26 paychecks per year.

The calculator first determines your gross pay, then estimates taxes using the federal and state percentages you provide. These amounts are subtracted to calculate your net pay.

Understanding your biweekly net pay helps with budgeting recurring expenses such as rent, utilities, loan payments, and savings contributions.

All calculations are performed locally in your browser for speed and privacy, and inputs can be adjusted at any time for instant results.
